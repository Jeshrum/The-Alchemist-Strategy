## Core Concepts

### SNR Levels (Support & Resistance)
Use the **line chart** (close prices only) to identify all levels.

| Pattern | Definition | Level Placement |
|---|---|---|
| A-formation | Bullish candle → Bearish candle | Closing price of the bullish candle = Resistance |
| V-formation | Bearish candle → Bullish candle | Closing price of the bearish candle = Support |
| Open-Close Level | Two consecutive same-direction candles | Closing price of the FIRST candle = key level |
| RBS | Resistance Broken Support | Old resistance becomes new support after breakout |
| SBR | Support Broken Resistance | Old support becomes new resistance after breakout |

### Story Lines (Directional Bias)

**Weekly Bearish Story Line STARTS when:**
- Weekly candle closes below a weekly resistance (A-formation or open-close level)
- AND same week: Daily gives a bearish breakout or bearish QM

**Weekly Bearish Story Line ENDS when:**
- Weekly rejects a weekly support
- AND same week: Daily gives a bullish breakout or bullish QM

**Daily Bearish Story Line STARTS when:**
- Daily closes below a daily resistance
- AND same day: H4 gives a bearish breakout or bearish QM

**Daily Bullish Story Line STARTS when:**
- Daily closes above a daily support
- AND same day: H4 gives a bullish breakout or bullish QM

**Conflict rule:** Weekly is senior. If weekly is bullish but daily is bearish → daily sells are intraday only, longs remain priority.

### QM (Quasimodo)
Pattern: LH → LL → LH → LL → HH (bearish QM reversal)
The HH breakout confirms story line start/end.

### Obstacles
In any story line, price doesn't move straight to target.
- Weekly bearish story line → Daily supports are obstacles (expect bounces)
- Daily bearish story line → H4 supports are obstacles

### Decision Points
Advanced concept — open-close levels the market returns to repeatedly. Treat as high-importance zones.

## Entry Rules — Confirmation Setup (Prop Firm Only)

**Step-by-step checklist:**

1. ✅ Story line is clear (weekly + daily bias confirmed)
2. ✅ Asia session is over (skip first 2 H4 candles of the day — 00:00–06:00 UTC)
3. ✅ H4 candle closes rejecting an H4 zone (resistance for sells, support for buys)
4. ✅ H1 breaks an open-close level OR H1 breaks structure in trade direction
5. ✅ Entry timing aligns with a kill zone (see sessions below)
6. ✅ Trend line confluence at the entry zone (continuation or break-and-retest TL)
   - If no trend line → medium-risk entry, reduce position size
7. Place limit order at the H4 zone refined to nearest H1 level

**Entry types:**
| Type | Conditions | Risk Level | Use On Prop? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low-risk | All 6 steps confirmed | Full size | ✅ Yes |
| Medium-risk | Steps 1-5, no trend line | Reduced size | ⚠️ Careful |
| High-risk | Direct entry at daily/weekly zone | Very small | ❌ No |

## Stop Loss & Take Profit

- **Stop Loss:** 20–25 pips above resistance (sells) or below support (buys)
- **Take Profit:** 100 pips = 1:5 R:R
- **Move to break-even** once trade is comfortably in profit
- **Minimum target:** 1:3 acceptable. 1:5 is standard.
- **Swing entries:** Hold to next weekly/daily zone (200–400+ pips)
- **Fridays:** Close trades 2 hours before NY close

## Risk Management

- Risk 1% per trade on confirmation entries → target 5% (1:5)
- Never risk same % on high-risk setups as low-risk
- 2–3 days with no setup is normal — do not force entries
- Master one setup, then scale capital — never change the system

## Trend Lines

| Type | Description | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Continuation | Connects swing lows in uptrend (or highs in downtrend) | ⭐⭐⭐ Best |
| Break & Retest | Broken TL now acting as support/resistance in opposite direction | ⭐⭐⭐ Best |
| Reversal | Connects opposite swing points | ⭐ Last resort |
| EQ Point | Two trend lines overlap at same zone | ⭐⭐⭐ Strongest |

**Rules:**
- Only draw from origin points of a trend
- Do NOT connect random two points
- If no valid trend line exists → medium-risk entry only

## What NOT to Do

1. No M1, M5, M15, M30 — ever
2. No trading during Asia session
3. No trades without a confirmed story line
4. No over-refining zones (stay within 20–30 pips of H4 zone)
5. No high-risk entries on prop firm accounts
6. No trading both directions simultaneously
7. No scrolling far left for old zones
8. No SMC concepts (order blocks, FVGs, ranges) — only session liquidity
9. No chasing missed entries
10. No closing confirmation entries early (let them run to 1:5)
11. No random trend lines — only from origin points
12. No changing the setup once mastered

## SMC Element (Only One Used)

**Session Liquidity:**
- Previous day high/low
- Previous week high/low
- Asian session high/low

If liquidity sits just beyond the entry zone (above sell zone, below buy zone) → zone is stronger.
